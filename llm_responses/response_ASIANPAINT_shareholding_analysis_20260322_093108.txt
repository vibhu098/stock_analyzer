<h4>1. Shareholding Composition Overview</h4>

<p>Asian Paints Limited maintains one of the most stable and well-distributed shareholding structures among large-cap Indian consumer companies. The Choksey family-led promoter group has historically maintained a controlling stake of approximately 52-53%, providing strong governance continuity. The combination of high promoter confidence, significant institutional participation, and a broad retail base makes Asian Paints a structurally sound holding from an ownership perspective.</p>

<p>The most significant structural shift over FY24 has been the rotation from foreign to domestic institutional ownership. FIIs have reduced exposure by approximately 130 basis points year-on-year, while domestic mutual funds have absorbed this selling, increasing their stake by approximately 120 basis points. This rotation reflects both global risk-off sentiment toward emerging market consumer staples and increasing domestic SIP-driven inflows into large-cap equity funds that hold Asian Paints as a core position.</p>

<p>The absolute absence of promoter pledge across all five years is an exceptionally strong governance signal. Unlike several peers in the broader consumer and industrials space, the Choksey family promoter group has never encumbered their shareholding, indicating no financial stress at the promoter level and complete alignment of interest with minority shareholders. The free float market cap decline in FY24 reflects the stock's price correction from peak valuations rather than any structural change in ownership, as the promoter stake has remained precisely constant at 52.63% for multiple years.</p>

<h4>3. Major Shareholders and Institutional Ownership Detail</h4>

<h5>3a. Promoter Group Composition</h5>

<p>The promoter group of Asian Paints comprises the founding Choksey family and associated entities. The promoter holding has been remarkably stable, a hallmark of the company's governance philosophy. The family's long-term commitment to the business is evidenced by the complete absence of any block sales, open market sales, or pledge creation over the past decade.</p>

<p>The four founding family groups — Choksey and Dani — hold approximately equal portions of the promoter stake, creating a balanced power structure within the promoter group that has historically prevented intra-promoter conflicts. This multi-family promoter structure, while sometimes a governance risk in other companies, has functioned smoothly at Asian Paints due to professional management being entrusted with operational decisions, with the promoter group functioning as long-term capital stewards rather than day-to-day operators.</p>
