<h4>3. Key Analytical Observations</h4>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Earnings momentum stalled in FY25:</strong> Net profit fell sharply to ₹3,585 Cr in FY25 from a peak of ₹5,315 Cr in FY24 — a ~33% YoY decline — driven by margin compression visible in quarterly OPM deterioration (from ₹2,000 Cr in Jun-23 to ₹1,123 Cr in Sep-24). TTM PAT of ₹3,775 Cr confirms the weakness is sustained, not one-off.</li>
  <li><strong>Cash flow quality historically strong, now weakening:</strong> OCF/PAT consistently above 1x (FY20–FY24 avg ~1.06x, ex-FY22 anomaly), signalling high earnings quality. FY25 OCF dropped to ₹4,166 Cr vs FY24's ₹5,757 Cr, tracking the profit decline. FY22 OCF/PAT of 0.40x was a red-flag year (working capital stress amid input cost spike).</li>
  <li><strong>Aggressive capex cycle underway:</strong> Fixed Assets + CWIP surged from ₹5,621 Cr (FY23) to ₹8,055 Cr (FY24) and further to ₹8,317 Cr (Mar-25), implying a major capacity build. This is compressing near-term FCF and coincides with the earnings dip — execution risk on new capacity utilisation is key to watch.</li>
  <li><strong>Dividend payout creeping up despite earnings fall:</strong> Payout ratio rose to 66% in FY25 (vs 60% in FY24), signalling management confidence in recovery but also reducing retained earnings at a time of heavy capex — a mild financial discipline concern.</li>
  <li><strong>Competitive pressure evident in margins:</strong> Operating profit per quarter has trended down from ~₹1,900–2,000 Cr range (FY23 peak) to ₹1,100–1,600 Cr (FY25), consistent with intensifying competition (Grasim/Birla Opus entry) and volume-driven pricing pressure, not just input costs. Recovery to peak earnings requires both margin restoration and volume growth.</li>
</ul>
