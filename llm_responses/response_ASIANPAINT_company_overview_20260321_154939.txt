<h3>Asian Paints Limited — Comprehensive Company Overview</h3>

<h3>1. Company Introduction</h3>

<h4>1.1 Primary Business Activities & Description</h4>
<p>Asian Paints Limited is India's largest and Asia's third-largest paint company, engaged in the manufacturing, selling, and distribution of paints, coatings, products related to home décor, and bath fittings. The company operates across decorative paints (interior and exterior emulsions, enamels, wood finishes, waterproofing), industrial coatings, and home improvement services. Founded in 1942 by four entrepreneurs — Champaklal Choksey, Chimanlal Choksey, Suryakant Dani, and Arvind Vakil — Asian Paints has evolved from a domestic paint manufacturer into a diversified home décor conglomerate with a formidable pan-India and international presence.</p>
<p>The company's flagship brand "Asian Paints" commands extraordinary brand equity across urban and rural India, supported by an unparalleled distribution network of over 70,000+ dealer touchpoints. Beyond traditional paints, Asian Paints has aggressively diversified into adjacent categories including waterproofing solutions, adhesives (through its SmartCare range), kitchen and bath products, and home services under the "Beautiful Homes" ecosystem — a strategic pivot designed to capture a larger share of the overall home improvement wallet. This diversification strategy positions the company not merely as a paint manufacturer but as a comprehensive home solutions provider.</p>

<h4>1.2 Key Products/Services & Revenue Mix</h4>
<p>Asian Paints' product portfolio spans multiple categories that collectively address the complete lifecycle of home construction, renovation, and maintenance. The decorative paints segment remains the dominant revenue contributor, accounting for approximately 75–80% of consolidated revenues, encompassing interior wall paints (Royale, Tractor Emulsion), exterior paints (Apex, Ace), enamels, wood finishes (Royale Aspira), and primers. The industrial coatings segment, operated through subsidiary Asian Paints Industrial Coatings Limited, serves automotive OEMs, general industrial, and protective coatings markets.</p>
<p>In recent years, the company has materially expanded its home décor vertical, including modular kitchens (Sleek International), bath fittings (Ess Ess Bathroom Products), and the "Beautiful Homes" service platform that offers end-to-end interior design and execution services. These adjacency businesses, while currently sub-scale relative to the core paint business, represent high-margin, high-growth opportunities that management has been investing in aggressively. The international business spans 15+ countries across South Asia, Southeast Asia, Middle East, South Pacific, and Africa, contributing approximately 10–12% of consolidated revenues.</p>

<h4>1.3 Company History & Market Position</h4>
<p>Incorporated in 1945 and listed on Indian stock exchanges, Asian Paints has maintained its position as the undisputed market leader in India's decorative paints segment for over five consecutive decades. The company holds an estimated 40–45% market share in the organized decorative paints market in India, a position built through relentless investment in distribution, brand building, supply chain excellence, and technology. Its nearest competitor, Berger Paints, holds approximately 18–20% share, underscoring Asian Paints' dominant competitive position.</p>
<p>The company's journey from a single-product paint company to a home décor ecosystem reflects a deliberate, long-term strategic evolution. Key milestones include the acquisition of Berger International (2002), the launch of the "Beautiful Homes" concept stores, the acquisition of Sleek International (kitchen solutions), and the entry into bath fittings through Ess Ess. The company is a constituent of the Nifty 50 and BSE Sensex indices and is widely regarded as a bellwether for India's consumption and real estate cycle.</p>

<h4>1.4 Current Size & Scale</h4>
<p>Based on the financial data provided, Asian Paints' total assets stood at approximately ₹26,661 Cr as of September 2025, reflecting a near 4x expansion over the past decade from ₹6,720 Cr in FY14. The company's book value per share stands at ₹199, and with an EPS (TTM) of approximately ₹39.35, the company remains a large-cap, blue-chip entity. The company employs over 8,000 people directly and supports a vast indirect employment ecosystem through its dealer and contractor network across India and internationally.</p>

<h3>2. Business Model & Operations</h3>

<h4>2.1 Revenue Generation Model</h4>
<p>Asian Paints operates a vertically integrated, brand-driven, distribution-heavy business model. Revenue is generated primarily through the sale of decorative paint products to a vast network of dealers and retailers across India, supplemented by direct-to-consumer services (Beautiful Homes), institutional/project sales (construction companies, government projects), and international subsidiary sales. The company's pricing power is derived from its brand strength, product innovation, and the sticky nature of its dealer relationships, which have been cultivated over decades through loyalty programs, credit support, and marketing co-investment.</p>
<p>A critical differentiator in Asian Paints' business model is its supply chain and demand forecasting capability. The company has historically maintained industry-leading inventory management, enabling it to serve over 70,000 dealers with rapid replenishment cycles. This operational excellence creates a structural cost and service advantage over smaller competitors. The company's manufacturing facilities are strategically located across India (Bhandup, Ankleshwar, Patancheru, Rohtak, Khandala, Sriperumbudur, Mysore, and others), ensuring regional supply chain resilience and freight cost optimization.</p>

<h4>2.2 Key Business Segments</h4>
<p>The decorative paints segment encompasses interior emulsions, exterior emulsions, enamels, wood finishes, waterproofing, and construction chemicals — this segment drives the vast majority of revenues and profits. The industrial coatings segment serves automotive, general industrial, and protective coating markets through Asian Paints Industrial Coatings Limited. The home décor segment, including modular kitchens (Sleek International), bath fittings (Ess Ess), and the Beautiful Homes service vertical, is the fastest-growing but currently the smallest contributor to consolidated revenues.</p>
<p>International operations span 15+ countries and are managed through wholly-owned subsidiaries and joint ventures. While the international business provides geographic diversification, it has historically generated lower margins compared to the India decorative business, reflecting higher competitive intensity and lower brand premiums in international markets. The company's investments have grown from ₹1,671 Cr in FY14 to ₹6,890 Cr by Sep 2025, reflecting both financial investments and strategic stakes in subsidiaries and joint ventures.</p>

<h4>2.3 Supply Chain Characteristics</h4>
<p>Asian Paints' supply chain is one of its most formidable competitive assets. The company sources key raw materials including titanium dioxide (TiO2), monomers, solvents, and packaging materials from both domestic and international suppliers. Raw material costs (primarily petrochemical derivatives) represent the single largest cost component, making the company's profitability sensitive to crude oil price cycles. The company has historically demonstrated the ability to pass through raw material inflation with a lag of 1–2 quarters, leveraging its pricing power and brand strength.</p>
<p>The company's distribution infrastructure — comprising company depots, carrying and forwarding agents, and a dealer network — is unmatched in the industry. Investments in digital supply chain tools, dealer management systems, and color-matching technology (Colour World tinting machines) have further entrenched dealer relationships and reduced working capital intensity over time. The significant increase in fixed assets from ₹4,643 Cr in FY23 to ₹7,931 Cr in FY25 reflects aggressive capacity expansion to support future volume growth and new category investments.</p>

<h3>3. Recent Significant Developments</h3>

<h4>3.1 Capacity Expansion & Capex Cycle</h4>
<p>Asian Paints has been in the midst of one of its most aggressive capital expenditure cycles in recent history. The CWIP balance peaked at ₹2,561 Cr in FY24, reflecting large-scale greenfield and brownfield capacity additions across multiple manufacturing sites. By FY25, CWIP had declined sharply to ₹386 Cr, confirming that the bulk of this capacity has been commissioned. This new capacity is strategically positioned to support volume growth over the next 3–5 years as India's housing and infrastructure cycle accelerates, driven by government spending on affordable housing (PMAY), smart cities, and infrastructure.</p>
<p>The commissioning of new capacity at the Vizag plant (Andhra Pradesh) and expansions at existing facilities represent a multi-year investment in the company's manufacturing backbone. While this capex cycle has temporarily pressured free cash flow and return ratios, it positions Asian Paints to capture incremental demand without supply-side constraints. The normalization of CWIP to ₹317 Cr by Sep 2025 suggests the company is entering a period of lower capex intensity, which should support free cash flow improvement going forward.</p>

<h4>3.2 Competitive Disruption — Entry of Grasim/Birla Opus</h4>
<p>The most significant recent development affecting Asian Paints' competitive landscape is the entry of Grasim Industries' paint business under the "Birla Opus" brand. Grasim has committed over ₹10,000 Cr in capital to build manufacturing capacity and a nationwide distribution network, representing the most well-funded competitive entry in the Indian paint industry in decades. Birla Opus launched commercially in FY24 and has been aggressively expanding its dealer network, offering attractive trade margins to incentivize dealer adoption — a direct challenge to Asian Paints' distribution moat.</p>
