<h3>a) Market Valuation and Price Metrics</h3>

<p>Asian Paints commands a premium valuation in the Indian consumer staples and paints sector, reflecting its dominant market position, brand equity, and historically superior return profile. The current Book Value per share stands at ₹199, and with a TTM EPS of ₹39.35, the stock's valuation multiples can be derived from prevailing market prices. At a current market price of approximately ₹2,350–2,400 (as of early 2025 trading data implied by book value and dividend yield of 1.04%), the stock trades at a significant premium to book value.</p>

<h4>Derivation of Key Valuation Anchors</h4>
<p>Using Dividend Yield of 1.04% and the FY25 Dividend Payout of 66% on EPS of ₹37.37 (implying DPS ≈ ₹24.7), the implied market price = DPS / Yield = ₹24.7 / 0.0104 ≈ <strong>₹2,375</strong>. This serves as our reference price for valuation calculations below.</p>

<h4>Trend Analysis</h4>
<p>Asian Paints' valuation multiples reached a zenith in FY21–FY22, when pandemic-era demand recovery and margin expansion drove EPS growth and investor enthusiasm simultaneously. Since then, a meaningful de-rating has occurred — the P/E has compressed from ~95x to ~64x (TTM), and P/B has fallen from ~23x to ~12x — reflecting both earnings pressure in FY25 (net profit down ~33% YoY) and broader market reassessment of premium consumer names. The rising dividend yield from ~0.6% to over 1% signals that the market is beginning to price in a more moderate growth trajectory for the company.</p>

<h4>Key Takeaways</h4>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Premium but compressing:</strong> Despite significant multiple compression since FY22 peaks, Asian Paints still trades at ~64x TTM earnings — a substantial premium that demands sustained earnings recovery to justify.</li>
  <li><strong>FY25 earnings shock is critical:</strong> Net profit fell ~33% in FY25 (₹3,585 Cr vs. ₹5,315 Cr in FY24), which has been the primary driver of multiple re-rating and price correction.</li>
  <li><strong>Book value steady growth:</strong> Book value per share has grown from ~₹105 (FY20) to ₹199 (current), a CAGR of ~14%, indicating strong retained earnings and equity accretion despite high payouts.</li>
  <li><strong>Dividend yield inflection:</strong> At 1.04%, yield is approaching levels that may attract income-oriented institutional investors, providing a potential valuation floor for the stock.</li>
</ul>

<p>Asian Paints has historically been one of the most profitable consumer companies in India, with ROCE consistently above the cost of capital and ROE well above sector peers. The company's profitability profile reflects its pricing power, distribution moat, and operating leverage. However, FY25 marked a notable inflection point, with margin compression driven by competitive intensity, new capacity ramp-up costs, and input cost volatility.</p>

<h4>Trend Analysis</h4>
<p>Asian Paints' profitability peaked in FY24, with ROE of ~34%, ROCE of ~42%, and net profit of ₹5,315 Cr — all multi-year highs driven by raw material tailwinds and operating leverage. FY25 witnessed a sharp reversal: net profit declined 33% to ₹3,585 Cr, and ROCE compressed to 27.3% from ~42%, the steepest single-year decline in the company's recent history. The quarterly data reveals that Sep 2024 was the worst quarter (net profit ₹597 Cr), suggesting a combination of volume pressure, competitive pricing actions, and one-time costs, with partial recovery visible in subsequent quarters (Dec 2024: ₹1,104 Cr, Jun 2025: ₹1,100 Cr).</p>

<h4>Key Takeaways</h4>
<ul>
  <li><strong>FY25 profitability shock is severe but appears to be bottoming:</strong> The Sep 2024 quarter's ₹597 Cr net profit was the lowest in recent memory; sequential recovery in Dec 2024 and Jun 2025 suggests the worst may be behind.</li>
  <li><strong>ROCE at 27.3% remains above cost of capital:</strong> Despite compression, ROCE of 27.3% still significantly exceeds a typical WACC of 10–12%, indicating continued value creation — just at a slower pace.</li
