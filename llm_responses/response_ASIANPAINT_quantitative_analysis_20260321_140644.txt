<h4>Valuation Trend Analysis</h4>
<ul>
  <li><strong>EPS peaked in FY24 at ₹55.41</strong> and declined sharply to ₹37.37 in FY25 — a ~33% YoY drop — reflecting margin compression and volume pressure from new competition.</li>
  <li>Book value has grown steadily from ~₹110 (FY21) to ₹199 (FY25), indicating retained earnings accretion despite higher payouts.</li>
  <li>Dividend payout ratio rising to <strong>66% in FY25</strong> (vs. 60% in FY24) signals management's intent to reward shareholders even as earnings moderate — but also limits reinvestment capacity.</li>
  <li>At a 1.04% dividend yield, the stock remains a <strong>growth/quality premium play</strong> rather than an income stock; valuation re-rating risk exists given the FY25 earnings decline.</li>
</ul>

<h4>Profitability Trend Analysis</h4>
<ul>
  <li><strong>ROE at 20.7% and ROCE at 27.3%</strong> represent multi-year lows — still respectable in absolute terms but a stark reversal from the FY24 peak of ~35–38%.</li>
  <li>FY25 net profit fell <strong>32.6% YoY</strong> — the steepest single-year decline in the visible 10-year history — driven by competitive intensity (Birla Opus entry), input cost volatility, and demand slowdown in urban markets.</li>
  <li><strong>Sep 2024 quarter was particularly weak</strong> with net profit of only ₹597 Cr (EPS: ₹6.23), indicating a structural pressure point, not just seasonal.</li>
  <li>Tax rate inched up to 27% in FY25 from 24% in FY24, adding further drag to post-tax profitability.</li>
  <li>Operating profit per quarter has trended from ~₹2,000 Cr (Jun 2023 peak) to ~₹1,123–1,324 Cr range in FY25 — a ~30–40% contraction in operating earnings power.</li>
</ul>

<h4>Growth Trend Analysis</h4>
<ul>
  <li><strong>FY21–FY24 was a golden growth phase</strong>: Net profit grew from ₹3,053 Cr to ₹5,315 Cr — a ~20% CAGR over 3 years — driven by post-COVID demand recovery and favorable raw material tailwinds.</li>
  <li><strong>FY25 marks a sharp growth reversal</strong>: Net profit fell to ₹3,585 Cr, essentially resetting earnings to FY22 levels. The 5-year CAGR on net profit is now a modest ~3.3%.</li>
  <li>TTM net profit of ₹3,775 Cr suggests <strong>partial sequential recovery</strong> from the FY25 trough — a tentative positive signal.</li>
  <li>Historical 10-year EPS CAGR of ~10–12% reflects Asian Paints' long-term compounding quality, though the near-term trajectory is challenged.</li>
  <li>Asset base grew at ~14.9% CAGR (3-yr) even as profits declined — indicating <strong>capital being deployed ahead of demand</strong>, which may weigh on near-term return ratios.</li>
</ul>
