<h3>Asian Paints Limited (ASIANPAINT) — Comprehensive Valuation & Recommendation</h3>
<p><em>Equity Research | Paints & Coatings Sector | NSE: ASIANPAINT | Report Date: June 2025</em></p>

<hr>

<h3>1. Valuation Methodology</h3>

<p>Asian Paints is India's largest decorative paints company with a dominant ~50% market share in organized decorative paints, a robust distribution network of 75,000+ dealer touchpoints, and a premium brand franchise built over seven decades. Valuing Asian Paints requires a multi-method approach given its blend of high-quality compounding characteristics, near-term earnings pressure from competitive intensity, and long-term structural growth tailwinds from India's housing and infrastructure cycle. We employ four valuation frameworks below, each calibrated to publicly available financial data and sector benchmarks.</p>

<h4>a) P/E Multiple Valuation</h4>

<p>The P/E approach is the primary valuation anchor for Asian Paints given the market's long history of pricing the stock on earnings multiples. The company has historically commanded a significant premium to peers owing to its superior return profile, brand moat, and execution track record. However, the entry of Grasim Industries (Birla Opus) into decorative paints, margin normalization post raw-material tailwinds, and a cyclical demand slowdown in FY24–FY25 have compressed near-term earnings growth and warrant a modest discount to peak historical multiples.</p>

Note that FY25 earnings are expected to have declined meaningfully (management commentary and Q3FY25 results indicate ~15–20% PAT decline YoY) due to competitive pricing pressure and demand softness. <p><strong>Rationale for Premium/Discount:</strong> Asian Paints has historically traded at a 25–35% premium to peers on account of its superior EBITDA margins (~18–20% vs. sector ~12–15%), unmatched distribution reach, strong brand equity, and consistent free cash flow generation. However, in the current environment, we apply a <strong>modest discount to its historical premium</strong> — assigning a fair P/E of <strong>48x FY26E EPS</strong> — reflecting: (1) competitive disruption from Birla Opus, (2) near-term earnings contraction, and (3) slower volume recovery. This is still a premium to peers but below Asian Paints' own 5-year average of ~60x, reflecting the structural shift in competitive dynamics.</p>

<p><strong>P/E Fair Value = 48x × ₹54 (FY26E EPS) = ₹2,592</strong></p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Key Takeaway:</strong> Even at a 20% discount to its 5-year average P/E of 60x, Asian Paints commands a premium to peers. The fair value of ~₹2,592 implies limited upside from current levels (~₹2,350), confirming the stock is fairly valued to mildly overvalued on near-term earnings.</li>
  <li>The compression in forward P/E from ~78x (FY21) to ~42–44x currently reflects both earnings normalization and market reassessment of competitive moat, which we believe is partially, but not fully, priced in.</li>
  <li>A re-rating to 55–60x would require clear evidence of competitive stabilization and volume growth recovery above 10% — conditions we do not see materializing within 12 months.</li>
</ul>

<h4>b) Price-to-Book (P/B) Valuation</h4>

<p>The P/B approach is particularly relevant for high-ROE franchises like Asian Paints, where book value is continuously compounded by retained earnings. The Gordon Growth Model-derived justified P/B = ROE × (1 − Payout Ratio) / (Cost of Equity − Growth Rate) provides a theoretically sound anchor. Asian Paints' ROE has been structurally high, averaging 28–35% over the past five years, though it has moderated slightly in FY24 due to capital-intensive international expansion and home décor investments.</p>

<p><strong>ROE-Justified P/B Calculation:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Sustainable ROE: <strong>30%</strong> (slightly below FY24 peak, accounting for competitive pressure)</li>
  <li>Cost of Equity (Ke): <strong>11%</strong> (Risk-free rate ~7.1% + Beta ~0.65 × ERP ~6% = ~11%)</li>
  <li>Long-term sustainable growth rate (g): <strong>8%</strong></li>
  <li>Justified P/B = ROE / (Ke − g) = 30% / (11% − 8%) = <strong>10.0x</strong></li>
  <li>FY26E BVPS (estimated): ~₹210 (growing at ~12% from FY24 base of ₹188)</li>
  <li><strong>P/B Fair Value = 10.0x × ₹210 = ₹2,100</strong></li>
</ul>

The ROE-justified P/B fair value of ₹2,100 implies meaningful downside from current levels, making this the most conservative of our four valuation methods. <ul>
  <li><strong>Key Takeaway:</strong> The P/B model signals overvaluation at current prices. For the stock to justify its current P/B of ~12.5x, Asian Paints would need to sustain ROE above 35% — a scenario that appears challenging given new entrant competition and pricing pressure.</li>
  <li>The declining trend in market P/B (from 22x in FY21 to ~12.5x currently) reflects the market's gradual reassessment of the competitive moat, a process we believe is still incomplete.</li>
</ul>

<h4>c) Dividend Discount Model (DDM)</h4>

<p>Asian Paints has been a consistent and growing dividend payer, with a payout ratio averaging ~55–62% over five years. The DDM is applicable given the predictability of dividends, though the relatively low current yield limits this model's weight in our blended valuation. We use a two-stage DDM: a higher growth phase (FY25–FY28) and a terminal growth phase thereafter.</p>
