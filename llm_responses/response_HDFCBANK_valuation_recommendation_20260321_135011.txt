<h4>Investment Rationale</h4>

<ol>
  <li><strong>Post-Merger Normalization Underway — Earnings Recovery Visible:</strong> HDFC Bank's merger with HDFC Ltd (July 2023) created significant short-term NIM compression (~3.4–3.5% vs. pre-merger ~4.1%) due to high-cost borrowings absorbed from HDFC Ltd. As legacy HDFC Ltd borrowings (carrying 7.5–8.5% cost) progressively mature and are replaced by lower-cost deposits, NIM is expected to recover to 3.8–4.0% by FY27, adding 200–250 bps to RoA trajectory and driving 14–16% EPS CAGR over FY25–FY27.</li>
  <li><strong>Valuation at Significant Discount to Historical Averages — Margin of Safety Present:</strong> HDFC Bank currently trades at ~2.6x P/B (FY25E BVPS ~₹750) versus its 5-year pre-merger average of 3.5–4.0x P/B. The stock also trades at ~23x trailing P/E vs. ICICI Bank at 20x — however, HDFC Bank's superior franchise, lower credit costs (~0.4% vs. sector ~1.0%), and CASA ratio of ~38% justify a structural premium. The current valuation offers a rare entry point into a tier-1 franchise at compressed multiples.</li>
  <li><strong>Structural Growth Drivers Intact — India's Credit Penetration Story:</strong> India's credit-to-GDP ratio of ~57% (vs. 130%+ in developed markets) provides a long structural runway. HDFC Bank, with 8,700+ branches, 21,000+ ATMs, and leadership in home loans, corporate banking, and retail credit, is best positioned to capture this growth. Loan book of ~₹26 lakh crore (FY25E) growing at 10–12% YoY, with a pivot toward higher-yielding retail and SME segments, supports NIM recovery and revenue diversification.</li>
  <li><strong>Key Risk to Monitor — Deposit Mobilization and LDR Normalization:</strong> Post-merger, HDFC Bank's Loan-to-Deposit Ratio (LDR) spiked to ~110% (vs. RBI comfort level of ~75–85%), forcing the bank to slow loan growth and aggressively mobilize deposits. While LDR has been trending down toward ~95–100%, full normalization may take 6–8 more quarters, capping near-term loan growth at 10–12% vs. the bank's historical 18–20%. This remains the single largest constraint on near-term ROE recovery.</li>
  <li><strong>Catalyst — RBI Rate Cuts and CASA Re-Pricing:</strong> With RBI having initiated a rate cut cycle (50 bps cut in early 2025), HDFC Bank stands to benefit on two fronts: (a) reduction in cost of deposits as CASA re-prices lower, expanding NIMs, and (b) potential uptick in home loan demand as EMIs decline, directly benefiting HDFC Bank's dominant home loan franchise (~₹8 lakh crore book inherited from HDFC Ltd). Each 25 bps RBI rate cut is estimated to add ~4–6 bps to consolidated NIM.</li>
</ol>
