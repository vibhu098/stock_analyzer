<h3>Asian Paints Limited (ASIANPAINT) — Comprehensive Valuation & Recommendation</h3>

<h3>1. Valuation Methodology</h3>
<p>Asian Paints is India's largest decorative paints company with a dominant ~54% market share in the organized decorative segment. Given its consistent earnings quality, brand moat, distribution depth (~1,60,000+ dealer touchpoints), and superior return ratios, the stock has historically commanded a significant premium to broader market multiples. We employ four complementary valuation methodologies to triangulate a robust fair value estimate, each capturing a different dimension of the company's intrinsic worth.</p>

<h4>a) Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Multiple Valuation</h4>
<p>The P/E multiple approach anchors valuation to normalized earnings power. Asian Paints reported consolidated PAT of approximately <strong>₹5,333 crore in FY24</strong>, which was impacted by elevated input costs (TiO2, crude derivatives) and a competitive step-up in advertising spend. We use a normalized FY26E EPS of approximately <strong>₹61–63</strong>, reflecting a recovery in EBITDA margins toward 18–19% as raw material tailwinds continue and operating leverage kicks in on a larger revenue base.</p>

<p>Asian Paints has historically traded at a <strong>10–15% premium to the peer average</strong> owing to its superior brand equity, distribution network, consistent free cash flow generation, and market leadership. However, with new competitive entrants (Birla Opus backed by Grasim Industries) disrupting the competitive landscape and near-term volume growth moderation, we apply a <strong>modest discount to its own 5-year average P/E</strong>, arriving at a target multiple of <strong>50x FY26E EPS</strong>.</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Normalized FY26E EPS:</strong> ₹62.0 (assuming ~12% PAT CAGR from FY24 base)</li>
  <li><strong>Applied P/E Multiple:</strong> 50x (vs. 5-yr avg of 62x; discount reflecting competitive headwinds)</li>
  <li><strong>P/E-Based Fair Value:</strong> ₹3,100</li>
</ul>

<p>The 50x target multiple represents a meaningful discount to Asian Paints' own historical average of ~62x, reflecting the structural shift in competitive intensity post Grasim's entry. However, it still reflects a premium to the peer average of ~35x on FY26E basis, justified by Asian Paints' superior ROE, brand moat, and execution track record spanning five decades.</p>

<h4>b) Price-to-Book (P/B) Valuation — ROE-Justified Approach</h4>
<p>The Gordon Growth-based justified P/B multiple links valuation directly to the company's return on equity relative to its cost of equity. This approach is particularly insightful for capital-light, high-ROE businesses like Asian Paints, where book value compounding is consistent and predictable.</p>

<p><strong>Justified P/B Formula:</strong> P/B = (ROE − g) / (Ke − g)</p>

<p>The ROE-justified P/B of ~5.4x appears conservative relative to the stock's trading history, which has seen multiples of 16–22x. This divergence reflects the market's willingness to pay a significant franchise premium for Asian Paints' brand intangibles, distribution moat, and consistent dividend track record — none of which are captured in book value. We therefore use P/B as a <strong>floor valuation check</strong> rather than a primary valuation driver, and apply a market-observed premium of ~15x P/B for our fair value derivation: 15x × ₹220 = <strong>₹3,300</strong>.</p>

<h4>c) Dividend Discount Model (DDM)</h4>
<p>Asian Paints has a consistent dividend history with a payout ratio averaging 55–65% of standalone PAT. The company paid a total dividend of <strong>₹21.35 per share in FY24</strong> (interim + final), implying a dividend yield of approximately <strong>0.7–0.8%</strong> at current market prices. While the yield is modest in absolute terms, the dividend growth trajectory is robust and well-supported by free cash flow generation.</p>

<p><strong>DDM Inputs &amp; Derivation (Two-Stage Model):</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Current DPS (FY24):</strong> ₹21.35</li>
  <li><strong>Stage 1 Growth (FY25E–FY27E):</strong> 10% per annum (supported by earnings recovery)</li>
  <li><strong>Terminal Growth Rate:</strong> 7.5% (nominal GDP growth proxy)</li>
  <li><strong>Cost of Equity (Ke):</strong> 11.5%</li>
  <li><strong>Stage 1 PV of Dividends:</strong> FY25E: ₹23.5, FY26E: ₹25.8, FY27E: ₹28.4 → PV = ~₹63.5</li>
  <li><strong>Terminal Value at FY27E:</strong> ₹28.4 × (1.075) / (0.115 − 0.075) = ₹30.5 / 0.04 = ₹763 → PV = ₹763 / (1.115)³ = ~₹549</li>
  <li><strong>DDM Fair Value:</strong> ₹63.5 + ₹549 = <strong>~₹612</strong> (theoretical; reflects only dividend income stream)</li>
</ul>

<p>The DDM-derived value of ~₹612 is intentionally conservative and reflects only the dividend income stream to an equity holder. It significantly understates intrinsic value because Asian Paints retains ~40–45% of earnings for reinvestment at high ROEs (~28%), creating substantial retained earnings value not captured in dividends. The DDM is therefore assigned the <strong>lowest weight</strong> in our blended valuation, serving primarily as a sanity check on dividend sustainability rather than a primary valuation anchor.</p>

<h4>d) DCF-Inspired Valuation (Simplified Free Cash Flow Model)</h4>
<p>The DCF approach captures the present value of all future free cash flows, making it the most theoretically complete valuation framework. Asian Paints generated <strong>operating cash flow of ~₹4,800 crore in FY24</strong> with capex of ~₹1,800 crore (including new plant investments), yielding FCF of ~₹3,000 crore. We project FCF recovery and growth over a two-stage model.</p>
