<h3>1. Valuation Methodology</h3>

<p>Asian Paints is one of India's most premium-quality consumer businesses, commanding structural valuation premiums owing to its dominant market position (~55% decorative paints market share), asset-light distribution network of 80,000+ dealer touchpoints, superior return ratios, and a decades-long track record of earnings compounding. The following section presents four independent valuation methodologies to triangulate a fair value range, with each approach cross-checked for internal consistency.</p>

<h4>a) Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Multiple Valuation</h4>

<p>The P/E approach is the primary valuation anchor for Asian Paints given its consistent earnings generation and the sector's well-established re-rating history. We use normalized FY25E earnings to strip out one-off margin disruptions from raw material volatility witnessed in FY23–FY24.</p>

<p>Asian Paints historically commanded a significant premium to peers (80–100x trailing P/E at peak), justified by best-in-class ROCE (~40%+), superior free cash flow conversion, and near-monopolistic market positioning. However, the structural entry of Birla Opus (Aditya Birla Group) with aggressive capacity and distribution investments introduces a credible competitive threat that warrants a moderation of the historical premium. We apply a 52x forward multiple — a 10–15% premium to peer median — reflecting residual brand moat and distribution superiority, while acknowledging the new competitive reality.</p>

<h4>b) Price-to-Book (P/B) Valuation — ROE-Justified Approach</h4>

<p>The Gordon Growth-implied P/B multiple is derived using the formula: <strong>P/B = ROE × (1 – g/ROE) / (Ke – g)</strong>, where Ke is the cost of equity and g is the sustainable growth rate. This approach is particularly relevant for high-ROE compounders like Asian Paints.</p>

<p>The P/B methodology yields a wide range (₹1,073–₹2,828) due to the high sensitivity of the justified P/B formula to ROE assumptions. The base case of 9x P/B on ~₹195 book value per share implies a fair value of approximately ₹1,755. However, the current market P/B of ~12x appears stretched relative to the declining ROE trajectory (from ~35% in FY22 to ~24% in FY24), suggesting limited upside from current levels on this metric alone. The P/B approach signals the stock is pricing in a significant ROE recovery that may take 2–3 years to materialize.</p>
