<h4>3. Key Analytical Observations</h4>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Earnings cycle has peaked and reversed:</strong> Net profit surged from ₹2,654Cr (FY20) to ₹5,315Cr (FY24), but collapsed to ₹3,585Cr in FY25 — a <strong>33% YoY decline</strong> — driven by margin compression (OPM fell from 23% to 19%) amid competitive intensity from new entrants (Birla Opus) and weak volume growth. TTM revenue of ₹30,042Cr is flat vs FY23 levels, confirming a top-line plateau.</li>
  <li><strong>ROCE deterioration is a red flag:</strong> ROCE peaked at 40% in FY24 but dropped sharply to <strong>27% in FY25</strong>, the lowest in 6 years, reflecting heavy capex deployment (Fixed Assets nearly doubled from ₹4,643Cr in FY23 to ₹7,931Cr in FY25) without commensurate earnings growth — a classic return-dilution risk.</li>
  <li><strong>OCF quality is solid but declining:</strong> OCF was robust at ₹5,757Cr in FY24 (OCF/PAT ~108%), but fell to ₹4,166Cr in FY25 against PAT of ₹3,585Cr (OCF/PAT ~116%). While conversion remains healthy, the absolute OCF decline alongside rising financing outflows (₹3,416Cr in FY25) is tightening free cash flow meaningfully.</li>
  <li><strong>Working capital has structurally worsened:</strong> Cash Conversion Cycle expanded from 49 days (FY21) to <strong>106 days (FY25)</strong>, with Inventory Days rising to 143 and Debtor Days remaining elevated at 39 — signaling either channel stress or competitive pressure forcing extended credit terms.</li>
  <li><strong>Valuation remains stretched relative to fundamentals:</strong> At a current P/E of <strong>56.7x</strong> on TTM EPS of ~₹39, the stock prices in a recovery that is not yet visible — 5-year stock CAGR is 0%, profit growth has slowed to 7–8% (3–5 year CAGR), and ROE has declined from a 10-year average of 27% to 21%. Risk/reward is unfavorable unless margin recovery to 22%+ materializes.</li>
</ul>
