<h2>Investment Thesis — HDFC Bank Limited (HDFCBANK)</h2>
<p><em>Equity Research | Banking &amp; Financial Services | NSE: HDFCBANK | Analysis Date: FY25</em></p>

<hr>

<h3>1. Executive Summary of Investment Case</h3>

<p>HDFC Bank presents a rare convergence of <strong>structural undervaluation and fundamental strength</strong> — India's largest private sector bank by assets trades at a multi-year valuation discount (P/E of 18.7x vs. its own 5-year historical average of ~22-24x) despite completing a landmark transformative merger, while the underlying franchise continues to compound earnings with disciplined credit quality and expanding distribution reach. The stock's 5-year price CAGR of just ~3% against robust earnings growth signals a compelling mean-reversion opportunity anchored by one of India's most durable banking franchises.</p>

<h3>2. Key Growth Drivers (Primary Investment Rationale)</h3>

<h4>Driver 1: Post-Merger Integration Synergy Unlock — Mortgage &amp; Home Loan Cross-Sell</h4>

    <tr>
      <td><strong>Quantitative Evidence</strong></td>
      <td>The July 2023 merger with HDFC Ltd. added India's largest housing finance book to HDFC Bank's balance sheet. The combined entity now carries a mortgage portfolio exceeding ₹7,00,000 Cr, making it the undisputed leader in home loans — a segment HDFC Bank previously had minimal direct exposure to.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td><strong>Market Opportunity</strong></td>
      <td>India's mortgage penetration remains at ~11% of GDP vs. 40-70%+ in developed markets. With urbanization, rising income levels, and government housing push (PMAY), the home loan TAM is estimated to grow at 15-18% CAGR over the next 5 years, representing a multi-decade structural opportunity.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td><strong>Company's Position</strong></td>
      <td>HDFC Bank now commands ~12-14% market share in home loans — no other private bank comes close. The merger enables cross-selling of insurance, mutual funds, credit cards, and savings products to HDFC Ltd.'s legacy 9+ million mortgage customers who were previously bank-agnostic.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td><strong>Financial Impact</strong></td>
      <td>Cross-sell monetization of the merged mortgage customer base could add ₹3,000-5,000 Cr incrementally to fee income annually over 3-5 years. NII uplift from the mortgage book, as funding costs normalize, could expand NIMs by 10-15 bps at steady state.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td><strong>Timeline</strong></td>
      <td>Early cross-sell benefits visible in FY26; full synergy realization expected over FY26-FY28 as technology integration and relationship banking deepens.</td>

      <td><strong>Quantitative Evidence</strong></td>

Management has explicitly prioritized deposit growth over loan growth — a deliberate de-risking strategy. HDFC Bank added ~1,000+ new branches in FY24, targeting semi-urban and rural geographies.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td><strong>Growth Potential</strong></td>
      <td>India's total banking deposits are projected to grow at 12-14% CAGR through FY28. HDFC Bank, with its brand equity and expanding branch network (now 8,700+ branches), is structurally positioned to capture disproportionate deposit inflows from Tier 2/3 cities where banking penetration is rising sharply.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td><strong>Execution Capability</strong></td>
      <td>HDFC Bank has historically demonstrated superior CASA (Current Account Savings Account) mobilization — CASA ratio maintained at ~37-40%, providing low-cost funding advantage. The branch expansion strategy mirrors the playbook successfully executed in the 2010s, when branch additions drove 25%+ loan growth cycles.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td><strong>Expected Financial Impact</strong></td>

    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td><strong>Timeline</strong></td>

    <tr>
      <td><strong>Current State</strong></td>
      <td>Post-merger NIM compression is a well-documented near-term headwind — the HDFC Ltd. mortgage book carried lower yields relative to HDFC Bank's legacy retail portfolio, temporarily diluting blended NIMs to ~3.4-3.6% from the pre-merger ~4.1-4.3%. This is the single largest overhang on near-term profitability perception.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td><strong>Improvement Opportunity</strong></td>
      <td>As the bank re-prices the mortgage book, reduces high-cost borrowings inherited from HDFC Ltd. (NCDs, external borrowings), and grows higher-yielding retail and SME loans, NIMs are expected to recover 30-50 bps toward 3.8-4.1% over FY26-FY27. Each 10 bps NIM improvement on a balance sheet of ~₹35-37 lakh Cr translates to ~₹3,500-3,700 Cr in additional pre-tax profit.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td><strong>Execution Status</strong></td>
      <td>Already underway — management has guided for gradual NIM recovery; legacy HDFC Ltd. high-cost NCDs are being systematically retired as they mature. The RBI rate cut cycle (if it materializes in FY26) further supports liability re-pricing favorably for HDFC Bank's deposit-heavy funding mix.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td><strong>Financial Impact</strong></td>
      <td>Full NIM normalization could add ₹10,000-15,000 Cr to annual NII over FY26-FY28, driving PAT growth acceleration to 15-18% CAGR vs. current ~10-12% post-merger muted phase. Cost-to-Income ratio, currently ~40-42%, has structural room to improve to ~38-40% as operating leverage kicks in on the expanded branch network.</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td><strong>Timeline</strong></td>
      <td>Phased recovery: Q1-Q2 FY26 (initial signs), Q3-Q4 FY26 (meaningful improvement), FY27 (full normalization).</td>
