<h4>Trend Analysis</h4>
<p>Asian Paints' valuation multiples have been on a meaningful compression trajectory since their FY21–FY22 peak, when the stock commanded P/E multiples of ~88–95x, reflecting pandemic-era re-rating and strong earnings recovery. The FY25 P/E has moderated to approximately 63x (TTM basis) as earnings declined sharply — net profit fell from ₹5,315 Cr in FY24 to ₹3,585 Cr in FY25, a ~33% drop — causing the market to reprice growth expectations. The P/B ratio compression from ~24x to ~12x signals that the market is reassessing the premium it is willing to pay for Asian Paints' franchise value amid intensifying competition and margin pressures.</p>

<h4>Key Takeaways</h4>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Valuation de-rating underway:</strong> The stock has undergone significant multiple compression from peak FY21–22 levels, with P/E falling from ~88–95x to ~63x (TTM), reflecting both earnings weakness and a structural reassessment of growth premium.</li>
  <li><strong>Earnings decline is the primary driver:</strong> FY25 net profit declined ~33% YoY to ₹3,585 Cr, the sharpest annual contraction in over a decade, making current valuation still elevated on an absolute basis despite the correction.</li>
  <li><strong>P/B still commands premium:</strong> At ~11.9x P/B vs. a book value of ₹199, the market continues to ascribe significant intangible value to Asian Paints' brand, distribution network, and operational moat — but this premium is narrowing.</li>
  <li><strong>Rising dividend yield signals price correction:</strong> Dividend yield improving to 1.04% from ~0.62% in FY21–22 indicates the stock price has corrected meaningfully, potentially creating a more attractive entry point for long-term investors if earnings recover.</li>
</ul>

<h3>b) Profitability and Returns</h3>

<p>Asian Paints has historically been a paragon of profitability in the Indian consumer discretionary space, with consistently high returns on capital and robust margin profiles. However, FY25 marks a significant inflection point where multiple headwinds — raw material cost volatility, competitive intensity from new entrants (notably Birla Opus), and volume pressure — have collectively compressed profitability metrics to multi-year lows.</p>
