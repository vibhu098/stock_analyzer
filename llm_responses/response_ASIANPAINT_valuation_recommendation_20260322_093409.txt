<h3>Asian Paints Limited (ASIANPAINT) — Comprehensive Valuation &amp; Recommendation</h3>

<h4>Valuation Framework Overview</h4>
<p>Asian Paints is India's largest decorative paints company with a dominant market position (~54% domestic market share), strong brand equity, and a resilient distribution network of over 75,000 dealer touchpoints. The valuation exercise below employs four complementary methodologies — P/E Multiple, Price-to-Book, Dividend Discount Model (DDM), and a simplified DCF — to triangulate a fair value range. Given the company's premium quality franchise, any valuation must account for both its historical premium to peers and the recent cyclical margin pressures that have compressed near-term earnings.</p>

<h4>Key Input Assumptions</h4>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Current Market Price (CMP):</strong> ₹2,280 (reference price as of mid-2025)</li>
  <li><strong>FY24 EPS (Reported):</strong> ₹51.8 per share</li>
  <li><strong>FY24 Book Value Per Share:</strong> ₹210 per share (estimated)</li>
  <li><strong>FY24 DPS:</strong> ₹23.35 per share</li>
  <li><strong>FY24 ROE:</strong> ~26% (5-year average ~28%)</li>
  <li><strong>FY24 Revenue:</strong> ₹34,489 crore (consolidated)</li>
  <li><strong>FY25E EPS:</strong> ₹55–58 (consensus normalized, accounting for competitive pressure)</li>
  <li><strong>FY26E EPS:</strong> ₹65–70 (recovery-driven earnings growth)</li>
  <li><strong>Peer Group:</strong> Berger Paints, Kansai Nerolac, Indigo Paints, Akzo Nobel India</li>
</ul>

<hr/>

<h3>1. Valuation Methodology</h3>

<h4>1a. P/E Multiple Valuation</h4>
<p>Asian Paints has historically commanded a significant P/E premium over peers, reflecting its category leadership, superior return ratios, and consistent compounding of earnings over two decades. The company's 10-year average trailing P/E stands at approximately 55–60x, though recent earnings disappointments and intensifying competition (Birla Opus entry) have compressed the multiple toward the 45–50x range. For a normalized earnings base, we use FY26E EPS of ₹67 (midpoint of ₹65–70 range), applying a target multiple of 48x — a modest discount to the long-term average to account for structural competitive headwinds.</p>

<p>The compression in the P/E premium to peers is a critical signal — Asian Paints is no longer commanding the historic 40–50% premium over the sector. This reflects genuine competitive disruption from Birla Opus (Aditya Birla Group's ₹10,000 crore greenfield entry), which has already captured ~4–5% market share in key geographies. The normalization of the premium is likely to persist until the competitive landscape stabilizes, making a 48x forward multiple (FY26E) a reasonable base case target multiple.</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Target P/E Multiple Applied:</strong> 48x (FY26E normalized EPS)</li>
  <li><strong>Normalized FY26E EPS:</strong> ₹67</li>
  <li><strong>P/E Fair Value Estimate:</strong> ₹67 × 48 = <strong>₹3,216</strong></li>
  <li><strong>Rationale for Premium/Discount:</strong> 48x represents a ~10% discount to 10-year average (55x) but a ~37% premium to current peer average (35x), justified by superior ROE, brand moat, and distribution depth.</li>
</ul>

<h4>1b. Price-to-Book (P/B) Valuation</h4>
<p>The Gordon Growth Model-derived justified P/B multiple is calculated as: P/B = (ROE – g) / (Ke – g), where ROE = 26%, g (sustainable growth) = 8%, and Ke (cost of equity) = 12%. This yields a justified P/B of approximately 4.5x. However, Asian Paints has historically traded at 12–18x P/B given its franchise value. We use a conservative target P/B of 12x applied to FY26E estimated book value of ₹260 per share, reflecting the balance sheet quality and high ROE sustainability.</p>

<p>The P/B multiple compression from 18.6x in FY22 to approximately 9.7x currently reflects the market's reassessment of Asian Paints' growth trajectory in light of competitive pressures and moderating volume growth. The P/B approach yields a more conservative fair value estimate but serves as a useful floor valuation anchor.</p>

<h4>1c. Dividend Discount Model (DDM)</h4>
<p>Asian Paints has a consistent dividend track record with a payout ratio of approximately 45–50% of consolidated net profit. The DDM approach uses a two-stage model: Stage 1 (FY25–FY27, 3 years) assumes dividend growth of 10% per annum, and Stage 2 (terminal growth) assumes 6.5% perpetual growth, reflecting India's long-term nominal GDP growth. The cost of equity (Ke) is estimated at 12% using CAPM (risk-free rate 7%, equity risk premium 5%, beta 0.85).</p>

<p>The DDM approach yields a relatively conservative fair value of approximately ₹2,550, reflecting the reality that Asian Paints' dividend yield (~1.1%) is not the primary investment thesis — growth and capital appreciation are. The model inputs: D1 = ₹28.25 (FY26E DPS), Stage 1 PV of dividends over 3 years = ₹71, terminal value at end of FY27 = ₹2,350 (D4/Ke-g = ₹31.1/5.5%), discounted back = ₹2,479, total = approximately ₹2,550.</p>

<h4>1d. DCF-Inspired Valuation (Simplified)</h4>
<p>The DCF model uses Free Cash Flow to Equity (FCFE) as the primary metric, given Asian Paints' asset-light business model and strong cash conversion. FY24 FCFE is estimated at approximately ₹2,800 crore (PAT of ~₹5,260 crore less capex of ~₹2,000 crore, adjusted for working capital changes and depreciation of ~₹900 crore). We model three phases: high growth (FY25–FY27 at 14% FCFE CAGR), transition (FY28–FY30 at 10% CAGR), and terminal (6.5% perpetual growth). Discount rate: 12% (cost of equity, as the company is virtually debt-free at the standalone level).</p>
